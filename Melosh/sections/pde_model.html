Acoustic-Gravity Equations

  • Mixed formulation for velocity and pressure unknowns:1 \[\footnotesize \begin{aligned} \rho \frac{ \partial\vec{u}}{\partial t} + \nabla p &= \vec{0}, \\ \frac{1}{K} \frac{ \partial p}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot \vec{u} &= 0, \Omega \times (0,T) \end{aligned} \]
  • Boundary conditions:
    • ODE type BC (sea surface): \[\small p = \rho g \eta, \frac{ \partial\eta}{\partial t} = \vec{u} \cdot \hat{\vec{n}} \text{ on } \Gamma_\text{surface} \times (0,T) \]
    • Seafloor velocity (boundary source): \[\small \vec{u} \cdot \hat{\vec{n}} = -\frac{\partial b}{\partial t} \text{ on } \Gamma_{\text{bottom}} \times (0,T) \]
    • First order absorbing BC (outgoing waves): \[\small \vec{u} \cdot \hat{\vec{n}} = -\frac{1}{\rho c} p, \text{ on }\Gamma_\text{lateral} \times (0,T), c = \sqrt{K/\rho} \]
  • Homogeneous initial conditions

Implemented in MFEM with high-order finite elements and RK4 time-stepping. 1Lotto and Dunham (2015)

Weak Formulation

  • Multiply with test functions \((\vec \tau, v)\) and integrate-by-parts the second equation; the ODE boundary condition is imposed weakly:
    Find \(\vec{u} \in \mathcal{U},\, p \in \mathcal{V}\), such that \[ \begin{aligned} &\int_0^T \! \! \! \int_\Omega \left[ \rho\frac{\partial \vec{u}}{\partial{t}} \cdot \vec{\tau} + \nabla p \cdot \vec{\tau} \right] \text{d} \vec{x} \text{d} t = 0 \, , \quad \vec{\tau} \in \mathcal{U}, \\ &\int_0^T \! \! \! \int_\Omega \left[ \frac{1}{K}\frac{\partial p}{\partial t} v - \vec{u} \cdot \nabla{v} \right] \text{d} \vec{x} \text{d} t \, +\int_0^T \! \! \! \int_{\Gamma_s} \frac{1}{\rho g} \frac{\partial p}{\partial t} v \text{d} \vec{x} \text{d} t \\ +&\int_0^T \! \! \! \int_{\Gamma_a} \frac{1}{\rho c} p v \text{d} \vec{x} \text{d} t = \int_0^T \! \! \! \int_{\Gamma_b} \frac{\partial b}{\partial t} v \text{d} \vec{x} \text{d} t \, , \quad v \in \mathcal{V}, \end{aligned} \]
  • Function spaces: \(\mathcal{U} = (L^2(\Omega))^d \times L^2(0,T)\),
    \(\mathcal{V} = H^1(\Omega) \times L^2(0,T)\) (+ homogeneous ICs)
  • Surface gravity wave height \(\eta\) recovered from surface BCs
